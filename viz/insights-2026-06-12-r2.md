## 1. Executive summary

- **gpt-5.5 posted the single-run top score (21/25)**; opus-4-6,
  opus-4-8, and all three Fable 5 efforts followed at 20/25. The quality
  ceiling is real but flat: five configs within one task of each other —
  and the 4-rep follow-up (§8) shows that 21 was the top of gpt-5.5's
  range, not a stable lead. Its multi-rep median is 19, tied with the
  opus pair; **the top tier is a statistical wash** (overlapping Wilson
  CIs). Do not report a strict #1.
- **haiku-4-5 is the CPC frontier at quality** — 18/25 at **$0.0042
  per correct**, 2.9× cheaper than gpt-5.5 ($0.0119) and 9.7× cheaper
  than Fable 5 (~$0.040+) while giving up 2–3 tasks. (CPC is
  cost-of-correct-*selection*, gated on a quality bar — it has no
  accuracy floor and under-taxes over-calling; see §7.)
- **The benchmark rerun surfaced a pre-described scorer contingency**:
  both parallel tasks systematically under-credited correct topical
  fan-outs that scoped the counterparty via a metadata `filters` key
  instead of a query token. The pre-run plan was authoring-discipline;
  seeing the filter-scoped fan-outs at scale, we shipped the
  pre-specified scorer change (PR #25, per-spec alternatives) and
  re-scored all 12 configs uniformly from recorded calls. 9 of 12
  configs were rescued on at least one parallel task; zero regressions;
  title-guess filters stay 0.
- **Axis E saturated on arrival** (12/12) and is reclassified as
  baseline floor. The adversarial reviewer on PR #22 predicted exactly
  this; the discriminator the axis bet on (colloquial period phrase →
  exact boundary) is below the current frontier across all tiers.
- **The decompose split reproduced exactly**: `vendor_renewal_decompose_01`
  again passes only on sonnet-4-6 and opus-4-6 (2/12, same two passers
  as the morning run). The pass set being the 4.6 pair — and not the
  newer opus-4-8 — is consistent with decompose-on-cue being a 4.6-era
  stylistic reflex rather than a capability gradient. Stated as a
  hypothesis: n=2 passers, one model family, and a black-box harness
  cannot observe a training-process cause. The adjudication is now
  closed (keep strict; present as a behavioral fingerprint, not a
  capability result — see §6 and CLAUDE.md decision 12).

## 4. Behavioral findings

### Distractor susceptibility — still all `summarize_document`

8 distractor picks total, all `summarize_document`, concentrated on
sonnet-4-6 (6 picks: 4× on `halverson_dispute_03`, 1× each on
`_04` and `stonebridge_term_01`); gemma and gpt-4o-mini take 1 each
on `stonebridge_term_01`. The near-miss shadow tool works; the three
categorically-wrong distractors and `search_history` get zero traffic
from anyone.

### Decompose vs. parallel — the split is a fingerprint

`vendor_renewal_decompose_01`: 2/12, **identical passer set to the
morning run** (sonnet-4-6, opus-4-6). Meanwhile 9/12 configs execute
correct parallel fan-outs on the tri task. The newest models
(opus-4-8, Fable, gpt-5.5) skip explicit decomposition and go
straight to retrieval. One reading — stated as a hypothesis, not a
finding — is that decompose-on-cue is a 4.6-era stylistic reflex
rather than a capability the ladder climbs; the harness is black-box
and cannot attribute this to a training process. Keep-strict is now
the closed decision (CLAUDE.md decision 12): decompose-first is the
only first move that reaches all three clauses of the ask, so the
2/12 is scored as correct strategy but presented as a generation
fingerprint, not a capability gradient.

### Effort-knob economics (Fable 5)

Identical 20/25 at low, medium, and high; cost rises monotonically
($0.806 → $0.845 → $0.882), so CPC strictly worsens with effort.
On this dataset the knob buys tokens, not net retrieval score — same
as June-10 (then 10/15 at all three efforts). It is not fully
behavior-neutral, though: Fable low fails `easton_amendment_03b`
while medium/high pass it (low instead passes `vendor_autorenew_02`).
The net is identical, the path differs.

### The 4.6 generation has a distinct profile

sonnet-4-6 and opus-4-6 are the only decompose-on-cue models AND
sonnet is the heaviest `summarize_document` consumer. Caveat: the two
signals overlap on only one config — opus-4-6 takes **zero**
summarize picks, so the summarize half rests on sonnet alone. The
decompose half is firmer (the 4.6 pair, reproduced across two runs).
A cautious read: the 4.6 pair leans orchestration-style; the 4.8/5.x
generation leans direct retrieval — suggestive, not established.

each; scoring and task JSON are unchanged.

1. **`vendor_renewal_decompose_01` — KEEP STRICT.** Decompose-first is

   `list_documents(type=vendor_agreement)` provably cannot reach the

2. **`easton_amendment_03b` — DEFENSIBLE AS-IS** (unanimous panel). It

   Pinehurst-scoped `list_documents` would surface the framework doc"

   no failing call even attempted that route — 8 of 9 failures fired the

   batch-aware scoring passes any model that does the renewal search
   *alongside* the listing.

## 7. Caveats & threats to validity

- **Single run for the r2 table; n_tasks=25; n_configs=12.** Adjacent-config
  rankings sit inside run-to-run noise — see §8 for the 4-rep CIs. Report
  structural findings (frontier shape, behavioral splits, axis postmortems)
  and tiers, not 1-task gaps between adjacent configs.
- **Effective discriminating n ≈ 12–13 of 25.** 8 tasks saturate at 12/12
  and several more sit at ≥11/12; the floor tasks are a deliberate baseline
  (decision 11), not discriminators. Frame as "25 tasks (~12–13 discriminate
  above the current frontier)," and exclude floor tasks from ranking/CI claims.
- **CPC is under-guarded by construction.** CPC = total cost / tasks with a
  correct call. It is undefined at zero-correct (no accuracy floor — gate it
  on a quality bar) and does not penalize over-calling beyond incidental token
  cost: 57 correct-but-over-called task-instances across the 12 configs scored
  1 while issuing more calls than necessary (gpt-5.5 the most frequent at 11,
  rate 0.44). Over-call rate ships as an unscored behavioral metric
  (`over_call_count`/`over_call_rate` per config) alongside distractor-pick rate.
- Parallel-task scores are offline re-scores under #25 from recorded
  `actual_calls`; single-call tasks keep runtime scores (their
  scoring rules did not change). `score_at_runtime` is preserved
  per config in the snapshot for auditability.
- The keyword-contains matcher (design decision 5) retains inherent
  leniency (substring topics); per-spec alternatives inherit it. Zero
  realized impact on r2 (all queries were on-topic); v2 hardening.
- **Latency is directional only.** Mean over 25 sequential calls, one machine,
  one afternoon; heavy-tailed (gemma median 24.8s, max 91.9s). gemma is **local
  Ollama on the author's machine**, not comparable to hosted-API round-trips —
  do not rank it against API latency.
- **`reasoning_tokens` is not cross-provider comparable.** Anthropic folds
  thinking into `output_tokens` (so the field reads 0), OpenAI reports it, and
  Ollama's value is a whitespace word-count. Use within-provider only; do not
  chart it across providers. CPC *dollars* remain apples-to-apples (billed off
  the response-reported model).
- **Fable 5 is frozen single-run.** Its API was suspended June 12–13, 2026 (US
  export-control directive); it could not be included in the 4-rep CI study
  (§8). Quote its r2 numbers as a single draw.

## 8. Multi-rep variance (June 13, 2026) — read before quoting any ranking

To put error bars on the single-run scores, each of 9 configs was run **4
times** on the same 25-task dataset (default temperature). Fable is absent
(API suspended, §7). Data: `viz/data/reps_2026-06-13.json`, regenerated by
`viz/aggregate_reps.py`.

Two quantities below, which must NOT be conflated:
- **score range (min–max)** — the run-to-run spread; the variance signal.
- **Wilson 95% CI** — within-trials sampling error on the pooled 4×25 = 100
  task outcomes. It is roughly constant (~±2 tasks) per config and is *not* a
  run-to-run variance estimate; it bounds where the true accuracy sits, not how
  much a single run jumps.
